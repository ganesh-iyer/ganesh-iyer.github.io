Having worked in UX Design for 5+ years, I'm now looking to master the full stack of Design.

To accomplish that goal, I'm currently learning research methods and working on front-end technologies as part of my MIMS graduate program at UC Berkeley.

So far, my graduate studies have helped me to:
  • partially overcome my code-shyness
  • understand the various quirks and irrationalities that designers must learn to design for
  • critically examine statistical research findings
  • speak a language shared by researchers, data scientists, engineers et al
  • channel my understanding of other fields by inspiring to make cool infographics like this
  • while continuing to hone my UX methods

I have previously designed products in the e-commerce, enterprise, lifestyle, learning and non-profit sectors and to expand my design understanding, I am currently designing the configuration interface for a security-sensitive product.

I have also volunteered as a journalist and have been a performing musician specializing in rock, blues, jazz and carnatic music for over 5 years.
