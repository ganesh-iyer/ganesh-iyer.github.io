Bio
In my 5+ years of UX Design experience, I have learned that the best way to understand a problem space is to design for it. I have designed for spaces like enterprise, e-commerce, lifestyle, security, non-profit, and e-learning.

In an effort to reinvent myself and master the full-stack of design, I'm currently studying Human-Computer Interaction and Data Visualization as part of my Masters in Information Systems program at UC Berkeley.

So far, my graduate studies have helped me to:
  • design effective data visualizations like this
  • improve my visual design skills
  • overcome my code-shyness
  • understand various quirks and irrationalities that designers must learn to design for
  • critically examine statistical research findings
  • speak a language shared by researchers, data scientists, engineers et al
  • channel my understanding of other fields through cool infographics like this
  • while continuing to hone my UX methods

I have worked with on-campus groups like Virtual Reality at Berkeley (VRAB) and BLUES (The Berkeley Laboratory for Usable and Experimental Security) .

My publications include a book and a patent .

In addition to this, I have volunteered as a journalist and have been a performing musician specializing in rock, blues, jazz and carnatic music for over 5 years.
