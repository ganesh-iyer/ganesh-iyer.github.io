I design as much through systematic and impactful small changes as through major overhauls. I aspire to design products that evoke a sense of intrigue, while conveying information.

Download Resume


Having worked in UX Design for 5+ years, I'm now looking to master the full stack of Design.

To accomplish this goal, I'm currently learning research methods and working on front-end technologies as part of my MIMS graduate program at UC Berkeley.

So far, my graduate studies have helped me to:
  • design effective data viualizations
  • improve my visual design skills
  • overcome my code-shyness
  • understand various quirks and irrationalities that designers must learn to design for
  • critically examine statistical research findings
  • speak a language shared by researchers, data scientists, engineers et al
  • channel my understanding of other fields through cool infographics like this
  • while continuing to hone my UX methods

I have also worked with on-campus groups like Virtual Reality at Berkeley (VRAB) and BLUES (The Berkeley Laboratory for Usable and Experimental Security) .

I have previously designed products in the e-commerce, enterprise, lifestyle, learning and non-profit sectors. I have co-authored a book as well as a patent .

In addition to this, I have volunteered as a journalist and have been a performing musician specializing in rock, blues, jazz and carnatic music for over 5 years.
